## II. In-Depth Direct Competitor Analysis

### 2.1 PowerLaw AI

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Founded | 2017 |
| Funding | Series B (Sequoia, Matrix) |
| Core Product | MeCheck Intelligent Contract Review |
| Technical Approach | NLP + rule engine, added LLM in 2023 |
| Target Customers | Large law firms and enterprise legal departments |
| Pricing | Customized for large accounts, non-SaaS pricing |
| Strengths | Deep expertise in legal NLP for years, rich precedent database |
| Weaknesses | Focus on large accounts, weak SME coverage; poor mobile experience |

**ContractGuard Counter-Strategy:**
- Avoid direct competition: don't touch large law firm market, focus on SMEs
- Differentiation: SaaS self-service > customized project implementation
- Mobile: WeChat Mini Program + Web dual platform

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|

- Depth superiority: LLM multi-step reasoning > keyword template matching

## IV. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Technical depth: Multi-agent collaboration + deep RAG + Annotation bridge, not simple prompt wrapping
- Scenario focus: Not broad but deep, only does contract review
- Product loop: Review → built-in editing → one-click modification → incremental re-review → print, entire process completed within system
- Cost advantage: LLM API cost far below manual review, per-use ¥19 with zero decision barrier
- Rapid iteration: SaaS mode, weekly releases

### Weaknesses
- Zero brand: New brand with no trust built up
- Legal compliance risk: Must continuously emphasize "assistive tool" positioning
- Knowledge base building: Need legal professionals to continuously maintain knowledge base

### Opportunities
- 90% of SMEs have no in-house legal, contract risk depends entirely on luck
- Domestic intelligent contract review market is virtually untapped
- Per-use pricing eliminates subscription resistance for low-frequency users
- Built-in editor + print avoids Word/WPS format compatibility issues

### Threats
- Big tech entry (Fadada already has e-signature user base)
- General AI (ChatGPT/Kimi) continues to evolve
- Limited funding: Initially reliant on self-funding

### Opportunities
- Market education period: AI concept is hot, high user acceptance
- Policy tailwind: "Digital China" policy promotes enterprise digitalization
- SME blue ocean: Competitors are all chasing large accounts
- Overseas opportunity: Chinese contract review export to Southeast Asian Chinese enterprises

### Threats
- Big tech entry: Alibaba Cloud/Tencent Cloud may enter legal AI
- LLM hallucination: One serious error could ruin reputation
- User habits: SMEs are used to finding familiar lawyers
- Regulatory changes: Uncertain regulatory policy for AI legal services
