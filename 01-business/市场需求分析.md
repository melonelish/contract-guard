## II. User Research

### 2.1 Research Methods

- In-depth interviews: 20 SME founders/business managers
- Survey: 500 valid responses (March 2026)
- Competitor testing: 8 domestic and international contract review products

### 2.2 Key Findings

#### Q1: "Do you have a lawyer review contracts before signing?"

| Response | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Never, sign directly | 28% |
| Only for high-value contracts | 45% |
| Every time (review myself) | 18% |
| Every time (have lawyer review) | 9% |

#### Q2: "What worries you most after reviewing a contract?"

| Concern | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Unsure if any risks were missed | 62% |
| Can't understand legal terminology | 48% |
| Unsure if the other party will accept modifications | 31% |
| Can't determine if clauses are "normal" | 27% |

#### Q3: "How much are you willing to pay for contract review?"

| Price Range | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Free (no budget) | 35% |
| ¥10-50/contract | 32% |
| ¥50-200/contract | 21% |
| ¥200+/contract | 12% |

#### Q4: "What features do you want most?"

| Feature Request | Mention Rate |
|---|---|
| One-click upload auto-review | 78% |
| Risk level labeling (Red/Yellow/Green) | 65% |
| Provide specific modification suggestions | 58% |
| Tell me "is this clause normal?" | 52% |
| Compare against industry standard clauses | 40% |

## IV. Competitive Landscape

### 4.1 Competitor Classification

| Type | Representative Product | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Traditional Contract Management** | Fadada, Shangshangqian | Large customer base, e-signature loop | Signing-focused, review is secondary, weak review depth |
| **Legal AI Platform** | PowerLaw AI, Huayu Yuandian | Judicial endorsement, rich knowledge base | Judicial-scenario focused, weak on commercial contracts |
| **General AI Tools** | ChatGPT, Kimi | Strong general capability | No legal expertise, no structured reports |
| **SaaS Contract Review** | LawGeex (US), Luminance (UK) | Global deployment | Not adapted to Chinese legal system |

### 4.2 ContractGuard Positioning

"The first contract firewall for Chinese SMEs" — filling the gap in China's intelligent contract review market, focused on **comprehension** (AI that understands clauses) rather than **workflow** (e-signature).

## VI. Market Entry Strategy

### Phase 1: Content Acquisition (M1-M3)
- Continuously publish "Contract Review Pitfall Guide" series on Zhihu/Official Accounts
- Keywords: "contract review", "liquidated damages clause", "breach of contract clause"
- Target: Monthly UV 100,000, conversion rate 3%

### Phase 2: Seed Users (M4-M6)
- Free version open indefinitely
- Select 50 seed enterprises for in-depth product refinement
- Collect review result feedback, training optimization

### Phase 3: Paid Conversion (M7-M12)
- Limit free version quota (3 per month)
- Launch Pro/Enterprise paid plans
- Establish partner channels (bookkeeping agencies, industrial parks)

### Phase 4: Scale Expansion (M13+)
- Private deployment version launch
- Industry verticalization (Manufacturing contracts/Technology contracts/Labor & HR specialized editions)
- Series A funding
