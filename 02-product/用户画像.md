## 2. P1 — Small Business Founder

### Basic Information

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Representative Name | Mr. Zhang, 32 |
| Company | A tech company, 25 employees |
| Role | Founder & CEO |
| Income | Annual salary 500K+ |
| Education | Bachelor's degree (Computer Science) |

### A Typical Day

```
09:00  Arrive at office, check emails
10:00  A supplier sends a 15-page procurement contract to sign
10:05  Open the contract, flip to page 3... payment terms... hmm...
10:20  Flip to page 8... IP clause... what does this mean?
10:35  Give up reading, WeChat a lawyer friend: "Help me review this contract"
11:00  Lawyer friend: "I have cases on hand, will look tonight"
18:00  Lawyer friend says nothing major
18:30  Signed and sent back
```

**The pain point is 10:05-10:35, those 30 minutes: he spent half an hour reading the contract but got zero conclusions.**

### Core Needs

- Know within 3-5 minutes whether the contract has major risks
- Unintelligible parts translated into plain language
- Trust AI's judgment (backed by legal citations)
- Affordable pricing (< ¥200/month for impulse purchase)

### Usage Scenarios

| Scenario | Frequency | Contract Type |
|---|---|---|
| Signing new supplier contracts | 2-3 times/month | Procurement / service contracts |
| Signing client contracts | 3-5 times/month | Sales contracts / service agreements |
| Signing partnership agreements | 1-2 times/quarter | Strategic partnership / agency agreements |
| Employee onboarding contracts | 1-3 times/month | Labor contracts |

## 3. P2 — Corporate Legal Counsel

### Basic Information

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Representative Name | Ms. Li, Legal Counsel, 28 |
| Company | A manufacturing enterprise, 300 employees |
| Role | Legal Specialist (the only legal counsel in the company) |
| Income | Monthly salary ¥15K |
| Education | LL.B., passed bar exam |

### A Typical Day

```
08:30  Procurement dept sends three contracts, needs feedback by end of day
09:00  HR sends new employee labor contract for review
09:30  Business dept follows up: "Is last week's contract reviewed? The other side is asking"
10:00  Start reviewing #1 — 30 minutes
10:30  Start reviewing #2 — 25 minutes
11:00  Start reviewing #3 — 40 minutes (this one has more complex clauses)
11:40  Reply to procurement dept
12:00  Lunch
13:30  HR contract — 15 minutes (labor contracts are templated, faster)
14:00  Business dept contract involves overseas clauses, needs to research...
16:00  Done researching, provide feedback
17:00  Write monthly contract review statistics report
18:00  Off work (early by comparison)
```

**Pain point: 50% of each day is spent on repetitive basic review tasks, leaving no time for complex contracts that genuinely require deep analysis.**

### Core Needs

- Automate basic review to free up capacity for complex contracts
- Standardized review criteria (new hires can get up to speed quickly)
- Ability to mark "Agreed" and "Disputed" (internal collaboration)
- Data statistics: how many reviewed per month, by type, risk distribution

## 4. P3 — Law Firm Attorney

### Basic Information

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Representative Name | Mr. Wang, Attorney, 35 |
| Firm | Mid-size law firm, 30 people |
| Role | Partner |
| Income | Annual salary 800K+ |

### Typical Scenario

A client commissions review of a financing contract. Mr. Wang needs to:
1. Read through the full document, mark risk points
2. Analyze legal basis clause by clause
3. Search for similar case precedents
4. Draft a review opinion letter
5. Present to the client in a meeting

Traditional workflow: 4-6 hours (foundational work accounts for 60%)

### Core Needs

- Not to replace himself, but to automate the 60% foundational work
- Generated reports can serve directly as opinion letter first drafts
- Support for on-premises deployment (client contracts cannot be uploaded to public cloud)
